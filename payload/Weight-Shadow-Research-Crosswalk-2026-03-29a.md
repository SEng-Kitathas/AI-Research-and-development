## SEAM 1 — TERNARY / LOW-BIT DISCRETE INFERENCE
**Status:** Supported

### Core takeaway
Low-bit and ternary representations are a real engineering seam for reduced storage, lower traffic, and hardware-friendly execution.

### Architectural implication
The project is justified in treating ternary or low-bit nativity as foundational rather than cosmetic.

### Branch impact
Supports RB-0, RB-2, RB-5.

## SEAM 2 — INTEGER-ONLY / CHEAP ARITHMETIC EXECUTION
**Status:** Supported

### Core takeaway
Useful inference and even training can live inside constrained integer arithmetic with careful scaling and representation discipline.

### Architectural implication
Cheap closed math is not fantasy. It is a legitimate design target, though branch-specific viability still needs proof.

### Branch impact
Supports RB-0, RB-2, RB-5, RB-7.

## SEAM 5 — NATIVE DISTILLATION / WEIGHT CONSUMPTION
**Status:** Strongly supported internally; directionally supported externally

### Internal evidence
The MHT thread repeatedly identifies consumption as the real bridge from clever substrate to usable intelligence. The Forge zip proves there is an actual ingestion architecture rather than pure hand-wave.

### What is concretely present in the zip
- Surgeon extracts functional blocks (“organs”)
- Scientist evaluates them with entropy/source/position/type heuristics
- Engineer maps them to radical-address/Hilbert-style regions
- Artificer enforces meritocratic writes with a >2% upgrade threshold
- The pipeline spec formalizes 7 stages: Download, Validate, Calibrate (Rosetta), Locality optimization, CRHQ, Ghost preparation, Radical export

### Architectural implication
RB-5 is no longer speculative. It is real, but pre-TQ2 in key places. The rewrite pressure is now obvious:
- replace or supplement entropy-first scoring with geometric resonance scoring
- replace pure hash locality with motif-aware or symmetry-aware locality
- export native TQ2/quaternion-ready structures rather than raw organ blobs alone

### Branch impact
Strongly supports RB-5; cross-links to RB-2, RB-4, RB-6.
